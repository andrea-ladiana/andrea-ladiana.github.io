Research Interests

My research focuses on heteroassociative neural networks, particularly Hopfield models, investigated from the perspectives of spin glass theory, human brain memory systems (cortical modules), and Hebbian learning in Machine Learning.

I have established new principles of cooperativity within these networks and pioneered the application of Federated Learning to Hopfield models, generating novel results using Random Matrix Theory.

A major focus has been extending classical frameworks into the "Modern Heteroassociative Networks," which establish a formal equivalence with Transformer Layers via a new "cross-banks split attention" mechanism. This framework has been successfully applied to Multiple Instance Learning, notably achieving state-of-the-art performance in tumor diagnostics based on immune repertoires.

My interdisciplinary work further explores the dreaming mechanism for Extreme Machine Learning, uses statistical mechanics to characterize local evolutionary theories (e.g., Ghirlanda-Guerra-like identities), and applies concepts from physics, such as Weiss domains, to model geopolitics. I also conduct rigorous statistical validation of algorithmic performance across various benchmarks.
